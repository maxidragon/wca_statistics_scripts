WCA Poland — chart descriptions
================================
All scripts accept --last N to restrict the analysis to the last N years.
Default (no flag) = all available history (Poland WCA started in 2005).


COMPETITION TRENDS  (competition_trends.py  →  charts 1–4)
------------------------------------------------------------

1. Competitions per quarter
   Counts how many WCA competitions took place in Poland in each quarter (Q1–Q4)
   of each year. Shows seasonality and whether the number of events is growing
   year over year.

2. Competitions per month — years overlaid
   Same as above but at monthly granularity, with one line per year on a single
   chart. Makes it easy to compare whether, say, June 2024 had more events than
   June 2023.

3. Cumulative competitions YTD
   Running total of competitions within each year — after each month you can see
   how many events have been held since January. If the 2025 line is above 2024
   already in March, that year is "ahead of pace".

4. Mean / median attendance per competition per quarter
   For each quarter, takes all competitions held and computes the mean and median
   number of competitors. Median matters because a few very large events can skew
   the mean upwards.


COMPETITOR ACTIVITY  (competitor_activity.py  →  charts 5–10)
---------------------------------------------------------------

5. Unique competitors per quarter
   How many distinct people competed in Polish events in a given quarter. One
   person is counted once even if they attended five competitions in that quarter.

6. Newcomers vs returning — stacked bar
   Each bar is the total unique competitor count for a quarter, split into two
   groups: those making their global WCA debut, and those who had competed before.

7. % newcomers per quarter
   The share of newcomers in the total competitor pool per quarter. Rising share →
   the scene is attracting more new people relative to regulars. Falling share →
   the base of returning competitors is growing faster than the influx of new ones.

8. Starts per person per year — histograms
   One histogram panel per year. X = number of competitions attended, Y = how many
   people attended that many. Most people go 1–2 times a year; very few go 10+.
   Red line is the median. For wide date ranges the panels are arranged in a grid.

9. Consistently active (3+ quarters in a year)
   Competitors who attended events in at least 3 different quarters of a given year
   — meaning they were active for most of the year rather than just once or
   seasonally.

10. Activity trend per year
    Mean, median and 75th percentile of competitions attended per competitor per
    year, shown as a trend line. Reveals whether the "average competitor" is
    attending more or fewer events as time passes.


NEWCOMER COHORTS  (newcomer_cohorts.py  →  charts 11–15)
----------------------------------------------------------

11. Newcomers per quarter (debuts only)
    How many people made their global WCA debut at a Polish competition in each
    quarter. Different from chart 6 — this counts only clean first-time debuters,
    not people returning to Poland after competing abroad.

12. Newcomers per month — years overlaid
    Same as 11 but at monthly granularity with one line per year. Shows whether
    summer is a peak debut period and how that pattern shifts year to year.

13. Cohort retention % (3 / 6 / 12 months)
    For each debut quarter: what percentage of those newcomers came back to another
    Polish competition within 3, 6 or 12 months of their debut. Recent cohorts will
    have incomplete 12-month data — annotated on the chart.

14. Cohort retention — absolute numbers
    Same as 13 but raw counts instead of percentages, with grouped bars for each
    window (3m / 6m / 12m) and a dashed line for the total cohort size. Lets you
    see the cohort scale alongside its retention at a glance.

15. Return count distribution
    Histogram of how many times each newcomer (across all cohorts) came back.
    The bar at 0 = people who never returned. The chart title shows their
    percentage. Answers the question "are one-timers the majority or the minority?"


RECURRING SERIES  (recurring_series.py  →  charts 16–17)
---------------------------------------------------------

16. Recurring series heatmap (series × year)
    Identifies competition series by stripping 4-digit years from competition names
    (e.g. "Warsaw Open 2023" → "Warsaw Open"). Shows all series that appeared in
    3+ different years as a grid: rows = series, columns = years, cell = competitor
    count (— = no edition that year). Series labels include the typical month of
    the event. Sorted by most editions first, then highest total attendance.

17. Top recurring series — attendance trend
    Line chart for the top 10 recurring series, showing how attendance changed
    edition by edition over the years. Useful for spotting which series are growing,
    shrinking or stagnating.


NEWCOMER ATTRACTORS  (newcomer_attractors.py  →  charts 18–22)
---------------------------------------------------------------
Note: this script is standalone and not included in run_all.py.

18. Top competitions by newcomer count
    Horizontal bar chart of the 20 competitions that attracted the most absolute
    newcomers. Bars are coloured by year.

19. Top competitions by newcomer %
    Same but normalised: top 20 by percentage of newcomers in the total field
    (minimum 30 competitors to filter out tiny single-event competitions).

20. Geographic bubble chart
    City-level scatter plot on geographic coordinates (lat/lon). Bubble size =
    total newcomers across all competitions held in that city. Bubble colour =
    average newcomers per competition. Top 20 cities are labelled. The distribution
    of Polish cities forms a recognisable map shape without a basemap.

21. Newcomers by calendar month
    Dual-axis chart: bars show average newcomer count per competition in each month;
    line shows average % newcomers. Reveals the seasonal pattern of when new people
    tend to show up.

22. Average newcomer % by WCA event offered
    For each WCA event (3x3, Pyraminx, Megaminx, etc.), computes the average
    percentage of newcomers across all competitions that included that event.
    Green = above overall average, orange = below. Shows whether certain events
    (e.g. beginner-friendly puzzles like Pyraminx) correlate with higher newcomer
    share. Correlation only — larger events tend to have more events AND fewer
    newcomers proportionally, so interpret with that in mind.
